Erie · Territory Outreach

Finding Nepali-speaking families not yet in our records

A short guide to the list and map, for anyone going out in this territory.

What this is

Each week, a tool checks recent home sales in Erie County for buyers with Nepali surnames, and compares that list against our territory records. Anyone who bought a home but isn't in our records yet shows up here — so we can go find them.

It updates on its own. New families appear automatically; nobody needs to maintain the list by hand.

The two resources

They're linked to each other — a click on either takes you to the other.

Using the map in the field

  • Every pin is a family not yet in our records. Tap a pin for the address, the buyer's name, and their likely family name.
  • Tap Directions in the popup for turn-by-turn directions from right where you're standing.
  • A blue dot shows where you are, so it's easy to use while you're actually out.
  • The map refreshes itself about once a minute — no need to reload it.

Claiming an address, so nobody doubles up

Before you head out, you can mark an address as yours in the Assigned column of the list:

  • Find the address and type your name in the Assigned column.
  • That row turns a color — and the pin for that address turns the same color on the map.
  • Anyone glancing at either the list or the map can see at once who's already planning a visit where.
  • Plans changed? Just clear your name from the cell and the color clears with it.
Example: once "Bikash" is assigned, his rows and pins share one color — his own, automatically.

After a visit

Once a family is added to our territory or confirmed as already known, mark Nepali? in the list. If they're Nepali, we'll know to get them added; if not, the row can just be deleted. Either way, nothing else to update, and nobody else will be sent there again.

In short

  1. Open the map or the list before heading out.
  2. Pick an address, type your name under Assigned to claim it.
  3. Use Directions to get there.
  4. Mark Nepali? once it's added to the territory or confirmed known.
